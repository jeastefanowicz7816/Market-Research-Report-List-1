<p><p>The copper cable market is primarily divided into two types: copper wire and copper cable. Copper wire refers to single strands used for electrical conduction, ideal for low-voltage applications. Copper cable, on the other hand, consists of multiple wires bundled together, designed for higher voltage and data transmission. Both types are essential in various sectors, including telecommunications, construction, and automotive, due to copper's excellent conductivity, durability, and resistance to corrosion, making them critical for effective electrical systems.</p></p>
