<p><p>The Central Chillers market is categorized into two main types: air-cooled and water-cooled chillers. Air-cooled chillers utilize ambient air to dissipate heat and are typically easier to install and maintain, making them suitable for smaller applications. In contrast, water-cooled chillers rely on water to absorb heat and are often more efficient for large-scale operations. They require a cooling tower, which can increase installation complexity. Both types cater to different cooling needs and environmental considerations in commercial and industrial settings.</p></p>
