<p><ul><li>Autoclave Process</li><li>Tubular Process</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Medium Density Polyethylene (MDPE) resins are produced using two primary methods: the autoclave process and the tubular process. The autoclave process involves polymerization in a high-pressure vessel, resulting in high-quality resins with excellent impact resistance and flexibility, making them suitable for packaging and pipes. In contrast, the tubular process uses a continuous flow method, allowing for higher production rates and enhanced molecular weight control, which is beneficial for applications demanding durability and chemical resistance, like industrial films and containers.</p></p>
