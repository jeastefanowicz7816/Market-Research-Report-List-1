<p><ul><li>Sodium Alginate</li><li>Potassium Alginate</li><li>Ammonium Alginate</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The dental alginate market comprises three primary types: sodium alginate, potassium alginate, and ammonium alginate. Sodium alginate is the most commonly used form due to its excellent gelation properties and cost-effectiveness for dental impressions. Potassium alginate provides enhanced flexibility and reduces shrinkage, making it suitable for detailed dental applications. Ammonium alginate, while less prevalent, offers unique advantages such as faster setting times. Together, these alginates cater to varying preferences in dental procedures, ensuring accurate and reliable results for practitioners.</p></p>
