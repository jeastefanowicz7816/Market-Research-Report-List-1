<p><p>The 3D cell culture hydrogel market is divided into natural and synthetic hydrogels. Natural hydrogels, derived from biological sources like collagen and alginate, provide a biocompatible environment that closely mimics the extracellular matrix, promoting cell adhesion and growth. Synthetic hydrogels, created through chemical processes, offer customizable properties such as stiffness and degradation rates, allowing for tailored applications in drug delivery and tissue engineering. Both types enhance 3D cell culture techniques, facilitating advanced research in regenerative medicine and biotechnology.</p></p>
